ASL Reference Guide

Learn about the hand gestures recognized by our system and how to perform them correctly. This guide will help you get the most out of Sign2Speak's recognition technology.

Supported Gestures

Thumbs Up

Make a fist with your hand and extend your thumb upward. This gesture is universally recognized as a positive affirmation.

Common Uses:
  • Approval or agreement
  • Positive feedback
  • Confirmation

Peace Sign

Extend your index and middle fingers while keeping the other fingers closed. Palm facing outward.

Common Uses:
  • "Peace" or harmony
  • Victory symbol
  • Number two in some contexts

Open Palm

Extend all five fingers with your palm facing forward. Keep your fingers together or slightly spread.

Common Uses:
  • Greeting or wave
  • Stop gesture
  • Number five

Point

Extend your index finger forward while keeping all other fingers closed into a fist.

Common Uses:
  • Indicating direction
  • Highlighting something specific
  • Number one

OK Sign

Form a circle with your thumb and index finger, leaving the other fingers extended upward.

Common Uses:
  • Affirmation or approval
  • Indicating "perfect" or "just right"
  • Acknowledgment

Victory/Peace

Form a "V" shape with your index and middle fingers while keeping other fingers closed. Palm faces outward.

Common Uses:
  • Victory celebration
  • Peace symbol
  • Number two

Tips for Better Recognition

Lighting Matters

  • Use good, even lighting to maximize recognition accuracy.
  • Avoid strong backlighting that creates shadows on your hands.
  • Natural daylight or diffused artificial light works best.

Clear Gestures

  • Make deliberate, distinct gestures with clear hand positions.
  • Hold the gesture steady for a moment for better recognition.
  • Position your hand at an appropriate distance from the camera.

Optimal Position

  • Center your hand in the camera frame.
  • Keep your hand 1-2 feet away from the camera.
  • Use a neutral background when possible.
